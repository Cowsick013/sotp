CHAPTER 1: THE MIDNIGHT CALL

The rain was falling in sheets against your office window when the phone rang. It was 2:17 AM. Only bad news comes at this hour.

"Detective, we've got a situation at the old Henderson mansion," the dispatcher's voice was tense. "The security system was triggered. When officers arrived, they found the owner, billionaire industrialist Robert Henderson, dead in his study. The medical examiner estimates time of death around midnight."

You grab your coat and badge, the weight of the raindrops matching the heaviness in your chest. The Henderson case wasn't supposed to end this way. You'd been investigating death threats against him for weeks, but the security was tight. Too tight for a random break-in.

As you pull up to the mansion, yellow police tape flutters in the stormy wind. Officer Miller meets you at the door, his face pale. "It's bad, Detective. The study's a mess, but there's no sign of forced entry. The security system logs show it was disarmed with Henderson's own code at 11:52 PM."

Inside, the opulence of the mansion contrasts sharply with the grim scene. Henderson lies slumped over his mahogany desk, a single gunshot wound to the chest. The murder weapon is nowhere to be found. On the floor beside him, you notice a torn piece of paper with what appears to be a partial phone number.

The lead investigator approaches you. "We've secured the scene, but there's something you should see. The safe behind the painting was open and empty. Henderson's assistant mentioned he kept important documents there."
